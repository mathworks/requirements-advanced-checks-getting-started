OVERVIEW

 

 

You are an engineer at Farnswerth Automotive and are on a secret project team developing a single-occupant autonomous vehicle. You are responsible for the adaptive cruise control feature, which should set apart the vehicle from its competition because it will allow vehicles to travel within a foot of each other. The team has just finished the desktop simulation and is moving quickly to 'hardware in the loop' testing in a state-of-the-art lab tucked away from the rest of the company. To save time and minimize development cost, you have decided to use an older cruise control design as part of your larger design. Before you can check the model in to the larger design, let's make sure the design is free of errors, meets requirements and meets company standards.

 

It is very important to evaluate a design early and often since identifying and correcting errors earlier in development is faster and cheaper. The MathWorks verification and validation tools give the designer a structure to work with, since the tools are integrated with the design environment to systematically validate and verify that a design meet the requirements and automate manual processes. MathWorks has an extensive set of tools to

  Identify and correct design errors

  Identify and correct incomplete implementation

  Ensure compliance with industry and company standards

 

In this example, we will walk through a simple requirements change workflow to demonstrate these capabilities.

This example workflow uses the following MathWorks products

 

  Simulink Requirements

  Simulink Check

  Simulink Design Verifier

 

This example assumes that the user has a prior working knowledge of Simulink and Stateflow.
